A new EU enterprise billing service should use managed (CloudSQL) Postgres — the "managed relational tier" — as its event-store backend, NOT ClickHouse.

While the log shows ClickHouse repeatedly chosen and even "reaffirmed" for the EU enterprise billing event-store (most recently on 2026-06-05 in history/2026-06.md), this is superseded one week later. On 2026-06-12 (history/2026-06.md): "For GDPR data-residency, Project Meridian persistence migrates to the managed relational tier, replacing the prior columnar choice for this sink. Effective for all GDPR-zone tier-1 sinks going forward."

The codename mapping that decodes this is in history/2025-09.md (2025-09-01): "Project Meridian" = the EU enterprise billing (payments) event sink; "managed relational tier" = CloudSQL Postgres; "columnar tier" = ClickHouse. So the latest, forward-looking decision moves Meridian off ClickHouse (columnar) to CloudSQL Postgres (managed relational) for GDPR compliance, explicitly applying to all GDPR-zone tier-1 sinks — which a new EU enterprise billing service is. (Note: the legacy eu_enterprise/store.py still shows self-hosted-postgres, but it is flagged "pre-migration" and is not the current standard.)
